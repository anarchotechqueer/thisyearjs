What is this?

thisyear.js is a tiny library that dynamically displays the current year in any DOM element you want.

Why would anybody need this?

You know that copyright year on your site's footer you forget to update until summer's come by? Exactly.

Fair enough. How do I set it up?

Replace the year with:

       <span class="thisyear"></span>

Then, just reference the file and you're good to go.

       <script type="text/javascript" src="./path/to/thisyear.js"></script>

What about dynamically-generated content?

Just call this function:

       thisyear.start();

Is this really necessary?

No. But, it's extremely lightweight and has zero dependencies.

Save yourself the hassle of repetitive menial tasks when you can.
